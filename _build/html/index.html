Theat Hunting Book

My work¶

Not hunting bad guys here, but their tools and strategies. What is important is to understand the Threat to better Hunt It Down before new victims come up. Over the years of working as a Cybersecurity Engineer, I’ve seen some compaigns, alerts, malwares, research papers, adversary patterns, ... All this gave me the idea of summarizing my knowledge and experience into a Notebook. And here it is, my very own Threat Hunting Book!

Personal Note: Threat Hunting (or Thrunting) is one of those activities in the technical field of Cybersecurity expertise that requires more consideration in my opinion. It’s often viewed as “part of” some other stuff, or considered as “best effort” only. My goal is not to “create” something dead impressive, but rather bring to the community what I’ve learned during my Thrunting trip, and just hope I can make it useful.

What is this Notebook for?¶

  • Present my previous, current and future work in the field of Threat Hunting & Analysis.

  • Contribute as much as possible to the community.

  • Provide lessons learned and small courses that can help the learning.

  • Share presentations material I’ve made during my conferences or guest talks.

  • Store Proof of Concepts scripts or adversary simulation attack patterns and detection / signature rules (YARA, Sigma, EDR/SIEM Queries)

Feel free to suggest anything using Pull Requests or Issues on my personal Github Repo for these Pages.
